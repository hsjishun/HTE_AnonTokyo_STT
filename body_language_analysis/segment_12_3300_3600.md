**Pedagogical Significance:** Moving to the front of the room is a classic nonverbal cue that establishes authority and signals a transition from independent group work to whole-class instruction. Pointing directly at the slide helps focus the students' visual attention on the necessary text.

**Pedagogical Significance:** Leaning over the desk lowers her physical stature, making her appear more approachable and acting as a collaborator rather than a lecturer. Pointing to the workbook encourages the students to rely on their reference materials, fostering independence.

**Interaction Style:** Her physical manipulation of the model directly mirrors her earlier hand gestures and her verbal explanation of "fishing reels." She is physically bridging the gap between the 2D presentation and the 3D activity.
**Pedagogical Significance:** This is a powerful demonstration of multimodal teaching. By using her own body to act out the process (reeling), pointing to a diagram, and then physically moving the students' materials, she caters to auditory, visual, and kinesthetic learning styles simultaneously, making a complex biological process much easier to understand.

**Interaction Style:** She uses minimal physical intervention here. By pointing to the materials but requiring the student to physically move them, she is guiding them to the finish line without doing the work for them.
**Pedagogical Significance:** Her encouraging posture and facial expressions create a supportive environment where the student feels comfortable attempting the final step. **Posture:** She is walking away from a group, but her posture immediately stiffens and becomes alert when the school bell rings.
**Hand/Arm Gestures:** At 35:48, she quickly raises both hands to chest level, palms facing outward toward the class in a universal "stop" or "hold on" gesture. She then lowers one hand and uses the other to gesture downward, indicating they should stay seated.
**Facial Expressions:** Her eyes widen slightly in reaction to the sudden noise of the bell. She then scans the room quickly, making firm eye contact across the classroom to ensure students are looking at her.
**Movement/Positioning:** She abruptly stops her movement across the room and stands near the center aisles, addressing the entire room.
**Interaction Style:** Her body language instantly shifts from a facilitating teacher to a classroom manager. Her sharp, sudden hand gestures directly contradict the auditory signal of the bell, communicating that class is not over yet.
**Pedagogical Significance:** The firm "stop" gesture and alert posture re-establish her authority over the classroom timing. This nonverbal communication tells the students clearly that the teacher dismisses the class, not the bell, helping to maintain order and focus in the final moments of the period.
